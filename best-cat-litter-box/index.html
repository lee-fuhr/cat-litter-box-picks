Quick answer

A good cat litter box isn’t just about convenience, it affects your cat’s bathroom habits, your home’s odor levels, and how much time you spend scooping. Choosing the right one means balancing your cat’s needs (size, entry style, privacy) with your own (ease of cleaning, footprint, budget). Start by considering your space: a compact box works for tight corners, while a larger or top-entry design can help with tracking and odor. Self-cleaning models save daily scooping but cost more and require power. The best litter box is the one your cat actually uses consistently and is easy for you to maintain.
